        <dd>SCP-3121 refers to two small, intelligent humanoids, about 10 cm tall, who look like imps or hobgoblins from old stories. They are a husband and wife, speak an ancient form of German, and are at least 800 years old, possibly the last of their kind. The wife, SCP-3121-B, has the dangerous ability to instantly travel by entering a person's brain, removing a piece of it, and then reappearing in another person's brain far away, often killing or severely injuring them. After being separated by containment efforts, SCP-3121-B violently escaped with her husband, SCP-3121-A. They are now contained together in a Swedish forest within an exclusion zone, and as long as no humans are seen by them, they remain peaceful, living a simple hunter-gatherer life.</dd>

        <dd>The "if-by-whiskey" concept refers to a rhetorical device where a speaker appears to take a firm stance on a controversial issue, but in reality, equivocates by defining the subject in two opposing ways, agreeing with both. This allows the speaker to appeal to both sides of an argument without committing to a single position. The phrase originated from a famous 1952 speech by Mississippi state representative Noah S. "Soggy" Sweat Jr., concerning the prohibition of alcohol, where he eloquently argued both for and against whiskey depending on how it was defined.</dd>
