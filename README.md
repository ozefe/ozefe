These infected humans are clinically dead, but their bodies are controlled by bees that have replaced their brain with a beehive-like structure. The bees inside these individuals then produce more aggressive bees, which the infected person can deploy from their body openings. These infected individuals can speak and act as if they are warships in a larger bee army, referring to themselves as vessels of "the Hive Authority." To contain this, one infected person is kept in a high-security vault, and any other instances are destroyed, with extreme measures like insecticide gas and incendiary charges ready for use if containment fails.</dd>

Initially designed for the elderly, hospital patients, and schoolchildren, it later expanded to families due to its ease of preparation and minimal waste. The production is labor-intensive, with factories in Thailand, China, and Vietnam, where workers meticulously remove bones before the fish parts are "glued back together" using the enzyme.</dd>
