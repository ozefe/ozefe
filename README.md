        <dd>SCP-4126 is a normal-looking white king-sized bedsheet with two eyeholes cut into it, which usually lies still. Sometimes, it rises on its own, becoming SCP-4126-B, a sentient, talking, ghost-like entity that enjoys "spooking" people by shouting "boo" and floating through walls. It can speak English, has an average intelligence, and acts like a playful teenager, but becomes immobile and distressed if placed on a mattress. Its active time varies with moon phases, but it remains constantly active and celebratory during the week leading up to Halloween, decorating the facility and hosting a party. It is contained by being neatly folded in a padded container and supervised when active, with candy used as bait if it becomes uncooperative.</dd>

        <dd>The Flagstaff War, also known as Heke's War, was a conflict in New Zealand fought from March 1845 to January 1846, mainly in the Bay of Islands. It started when Māori chief Hōne Heke repeatedly cut down the British flagstaff at Kororāreka to protest against the growing British authority and its negative impact on Māori trade and independence following the Treaty of Waitangi. The war featured several major battles, including the sacking of Kororāreka and difficult sieges against highly fortified Māori villages called pā, which showcased advanced Māori defensive tactics. Although British forces, aided by allied Māori, eventually captured the Ruapekapeka pā, the war ended with a negotiated peace, granting pardons to Heke and his allies and preventing land confiscation, highlighting the complex and often inconclusive nature of the conflict, which is considered an early part of the New Zealand Wars.</dd>
