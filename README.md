        <dd>The SCP-4049 article describes an extradimensional room with graves that translate to "hound" in Ancient Greek. This room is linked to two main events: SCP-4049-A, where male sport hunters in specific US states spontaneously combust during religious worship, leaving an arrow behind and causing a new tombstone to appear in SCP-4049; and SCP-4049-B, where a hostile, monstrous quadruped with human-like internal organs and the face of the most recent SCP-4049-A victim emerges from the room, attempting to escape. The Foundation tries to prevent SCP-4049-A events by lobbying against hunting and contains the emerging creatures in a heavily guarded corridor, which is also sometimes inhabited by an invisible, talking entity known as SCP-4049-2 that claims the creatures are its "pets."SCP-4049 is an otherworldly room found behind a steel door in Wyoming, containing graves marked with the word "hound." When male sport hunters in Wyoming, Idaho, or Montana spontaneously burn up during religious worship, leaving behind an arrow, a new gravestone appears in SCP-4049. Soon after, a large, hostile, four-legged creature with human-like insides and the face of the latest hunter emerges from the room, trying to escape. This creature is contained in a heavily fortified corridor, and the Foundation also works to reduce hunting in those states to prevent these events, while also dealing with an invisible, talking entity that claims the creatures are its "pets."</dd>

These actions, often driven by strategic interests such as combating communism during the Cold War or fighting terrorism in recent decades, have included military interventions, support for coups, interference in elections, and funding of opposition groups. Notable instances range from the overthrow of the Hawaiian Kingdom and interventions in Central America to the 1953 Iranian coup, the Bay of Pigs invasion in Cuba, and more recent conflicts in Afghanistan and Iraq.</dd>
