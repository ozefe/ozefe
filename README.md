        <dd>SCP-3656 was initially a basement room in Chicago that, once a month, transformed into a conference room for the "Displaced Governments Cooperation Organisation," a group of entities claiming to represent exiled governments from various realities, some with anomalous features. Foundation staff, posing as representatives of an Iranian exile group, would attend these meetings to gather information. However, during one such meeting, the Foundation team disappeared, and the room reverted to being an ordinary basement. Later, the entire area where SCP-3656 was located vanished, leaving behind a highly radioactive field, and documents from the "Displaced Governments Cooperation Organisation" were found, indicating that the Foundation's espionage activities led to a "firm and decisive response" against the "Persian state." The SCP is now classified as Neutralized, meaning it no longer poses an active threat, but the area remains a radioactive hazard.</dd>

        <dd>The Freedomites, also known as the Sons of Freedom, were a radical splinter group of the Doukhobors, a community of Spiritual Christians who migrated from Russia to Canada in the late 19th century. Emerging in 1902 in Saskatchewan and later moving to British Columbia, they opposed land ownership, public schools, and government regulation, advocating for traditional communal living. They became known for their extreme forms of public protest, including nude parades to express their rejection of materialism and occasional arson and bombings targeting their own property, schools, and infrastructure. This led to significant conflict with Canadian authorities, including "Operation Snatch" between 1953 and 1959, where hundreds of Freedomite children were forcibly removed from their homes and confined in a residential school in New Denver, British Columbia, an act for which the provincial government formally apologized in 2024.</dd>
