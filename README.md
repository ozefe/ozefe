        <dd>SCP-5088 is an empty swimming pool in an abandoned health club in Wigan, England. Despite having no actual water, anything placed inside it behaves as if it's submerged in liquid, with objects floating or sinking and people being able to swim but also experiencing difficulty breathing as if drowning. If a living creature suffocates within SCP-5088, it will reanimate briefly upon removal, but then die again, often violently, with subjects reporting terrifying visions of "something waiting" for them in the pool. The building containing the pool is sealed off and guarded to prevent anyone from entering.</dd>

        <dd>The Hyphen War was a political disagreement in 1990 over how to rename Czechoslovakia after the fall of its Communist government. Slovak politicians wanted a hyphen in the country's name, like "Czecho-Slovak Republic," to emphasize Slovakia's equal status, but Czech politicians disliked this as it reminded them of the 1938 Munich Agreement when Nazi Germany annexed territory. After several attempts at compromise, the country was eventually named the "Czech and Slovak Federative Republic," with specific spellings for both languages, before ultimately splitting into the Czech Republic and Slovakia in 1993.</dd>
