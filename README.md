This hatred can spread to others who witness the shunning, even if they didn't initially hear the word, making it highly contagious. The word itself is not reproduced in Foundation documents to prevent its spread. To contain it, the word is stored in written and spoken forms in a shielded locker, and its use is strictly forbidden without high-level authorization. Reversing its effects requires administering memory-wiping drugs to everyone who has been affected.</dd>

        <dd>This article describes "A Musical Joke" (German: Ein musikalischer Spaß), K. 522, a composition by Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart for two horns and a string quartet, which he cataloged on June 14, 1787. Commentators believe the piece is a satire, intentionally using harmonic and rhythmic errors to parody the work of incompetent composers, though Mozart's true intentions are not definitively known. The composition, which takes about 20 minutes to perform across four movements, employs various comedic devices such as dissonant horns, parallel fifths, clumsy orchestration, and starting movements in incorrect keys. It is also notable for one of the earliest known uses of polytonality, creating a sense of musical collapse at the end.</dd>
