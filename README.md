        <dd>SCP-2287, also known as "Mr. Headless" from Dr. Wondertainment's "Little Misters" collection, is a headless man who can still think, hear, and smell. While he appears headless to cameras and mirrors, anyone looking directly at him with their unaided eyes will temporarily see themselves as headless from his perspective, causing a feeling of disconnect. He doesn't need to eat or breathe and claims to get energy from "amplified photosynthesis." He was designed as a Halloween attraction, meant to scare and entertain people by sitting up and waving his stump. He is currently contained in a site and provided with Braille literature and incense upon request.</dd>

        <dd>The article "The Crucified Soldier" describes a widespread story or myth among Allied forces during World War I, claiming German soldiers crucified an Allied soldier, typically a Canadian sergeant, to a barn door or tree near Ypres, Belgium, around April 1915. While initial eyewitness accounts were contradictory and no body was recovered, the story was heavily used as atrocity propaganda, even leading to a formal complaint from the German government during peace negotiations. Despite largely being dismissed as an urban legend, a 21st-century investigation claimed to find evidence identifying the soldier as Sergeant Harry Band of the Canadian Infantry.</dd>
