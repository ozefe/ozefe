        <dd>SCP-5260 is a green and black bookmark that, when a person sleeps near it, influences their dreams. Initially, it causes pleasant, memorable dreams, but these quickly turn unsettling. After about eight nights, a helpful entity called SCP-5260-1 appears in the dreams, offering assistance and providing a beneficial effect that carries over into waking life, such as reduced anxiety or improved focus. However, people exposed to SCP-5260 become deeply attached to it and SCP-5260-1, often refusing to sleep without it nearby. If separated from SCP-5260 for more than 72 hours, their dreams become increasingly horrifying, leading to severe psychological and sometimes physical distress, including profound insomnia. The Foundation contains SCP-5260 by regularly exposing a specific D-Class individual to it, as this prevents its area of influence from expanding uncontrollably and seems to mitigate the negative effects on others.</dd>

        <dd>The article "Pornotopia" describes a concept in critical theory referring to an imagined space dominated by sexual fantasies and activity, encompassing erotica and pornography. Coined by Steven Marcus in 1966, this "pornotopia" is characterized by freedom from normal social restraints of place and time, operating instead on unconscious desires for enjoyment, where characters are often hypersexual and seemingly invulnerable. However, the concept has faced criticism for potentially biased literary sources and for overlooking themes of bodily failure and political commentary present in some historical pornographic works.</dd>
